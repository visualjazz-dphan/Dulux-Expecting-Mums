ROOM ONE

Dreamy baby

A nursery that is calm and soothing, with a little something special making the room unique. Using neutral tones and lots of white, a room is created that is relaxing and almost ethereal.

The scallop effect on the chimney breast uses Dulux Design Pearl™ as a highlight for a dreamy quality.

Colour psychology

Soft whites and warm, subtle shades can promote a relaxing, soothing environment, perfect for a Dreamy Baby.

tips & techniques:Scallop feature wall

Create your own scallop pattern on a feature wall in a few simple steps.

You will need: Three paint colours, paint brushes, stiff paper or card, a ruler, spirit level, and a pencil.

ROOM TWO

Vintage baby

Classic vintage style is timeless, this space has a quirky edge using lots of DIY imagination with the easy to create world map bunting and the painted “vintage find” side table.

Green is a colour that can be used for boys and girls, it symbolises nature and works perfectly with mid timber tones so often found in mid-century styles.

Colour psychology

Green can promote health and well being, it has a calming effect while being the perfect background for a retro style nursery.

tips & techniques:Revitalise a piece of old furniture

Vintage furniture pieces such as this small side table that was an op-shop find can be easily updated for nurseries.

Step 1: Remove the knobs and drawers and give the piece either light sand, or a wash with warm soapy water.

Step 2: Apply two coats of paint – we used the leftover Dulux’s Green Spruce wall paint or you can use a gloss sheen for a shiny finish.

Step 3: Once dry, replace the drawers and the original knobs to create a charming and affordable vintage feature for your nursery.

tips & techniques:Create a vintage
map bunting

Step 1: Remove the pages from an old school atlas (or childrens book, or wrapping paper).

Step 2: Fold one of the pages in half lengthways to create a light seam. Cut diagonally from the point of the seam out to the edge of the page – this gives you a template to use on the rest of the pages – simply cut around the triangle.

Step 3: Either hand or machine sew the pages to a length of ribbon then use tacks to hang it on the nursery wall.

ROOM THREE

Bright baby

A fresh white space is the perfect start, add bright pops of vivid colour to create a one-of-a-kind look.

A painted chair, a dip dyed curtain and a colourful blanket all tie into the diamond pattern feature on the wall, just enough interest to attract babies’ attention and adding an element of joy to the room.

Colour psychology

Small amounts of bright colour are a gorgeous way to introduce a happy and attention grabbing feature for baby to look at. Bright colours promote happiness and stimulate the imagination.

tips & techniques:Fun feature pattern

Step 1: Create a grid on your wall to represent the size of your diamonds

Step 2: Using a stencil mark out where you want each diamond to appear using the grid as a guide.

Step 3: Select 3-5 Dulux Paint colours and either mask each diamond or using the stencil, paint each diamond in the selected colour, repeat coat to ensure coverage

ROOM FOUR

Creative babies

A soft buttery yellow is perfect for a twin’s nursery. Diagonal stripes create a fun, graphic effect while splashes of other bright colours create a space to exercise their imagination.

Use bright graphic prints and fabrics to accessorise the room, alongside simple white furniture pieces.

Colour psychology

Yellow can promote concentration which is great as baby grows and learns, the colour is happy and cheerful and creates an inviting playful room, perfect for the Creative Baby.

tips & techniques:Striped feature wall

Create your own diagonal striped pattern on a feature wall in a few simple steps.

You will need: Two paint colours, paint brushes, paint roller, a ruler, a pencil, and masking tape.

ROOM FIVE

Sugar baby

Soft pink is grounded with a pale neutral creating a pastel tinted space that is delicately sweet. The oh-so-cute washing line feature adds an element of whimsy using babies’ petit clothes as a design element.

A soft sweet space, Sugar Baby will be relaxed and comforted while she sleeps and plays.

Colour psychology

Softer Pinks have a comforting effect, being both warm and inviting. Pink can promote empathy, helping to create a peaceful environment.

tips & techniques:Washing line wall design

Create your own washing line on a feature wall in a few simple steps.

You will need: Paint, paint brushes, a pencil, stiff paper or card, some small items of clothing, and some pegs (optional).

ROOM SIX

Nature baby

A neutral space is transformed into an urban forest, created with a feature of trees painted with shades in the same colour family.

Straight lines modernise the tree motive that uses stormy green-blue hues layered to give the motive depth. And no tree is complete without a watchful owl.

Colour psychology

Cool blues and greens have a restful effect, calming the body and mind and assisting to promote sleep.
